Top Tourist Places in India.

Explore India's most popular tourist cities and the top attractions within each one.

Agra | Jaipur | Delhi | Udaipur | Varanasi | Goa | Kochi | Amritsar | Rishikesh | Leh | Darjeeling | Hampi | Port Blair | Hyderabad | Chennai | Visakhapatnam

  1. Agra, Uttar Pradesh


    • Taj Mahal - Ivory-white marble mausoleum built by Shah Jahan for his wife Mumtaz Mahal; a UNESCO World Heritage Site and New Seven Wonder of the World.
    • Agra Fort - A massive red sandstone Mughal fort overlooking the Yamuna River.
    • Fatehpur Sikri - A well-preserved Mughal city built by Akbar, located near Agra.

  2. Jaipur, Rajasthan (The Pink City)

    • Amber Fort - A hilltop fort known for its artistic Hindu-style elements and mirror work.
    • City Palace - The royal residence with museums showcasing Rajasthani heritage.
    • Hawa Mahal - The "Palace of Winds" with its honeycomb facade of small windows.
    • Jantar Mantar - An 18th-century astronomical observatory.

  3. Delhi

    • Red Fort - A historic Mughal fortress and UNESCO World Heritage Site.
    • India Gate - A war memorial arch in the heart of New Delhi.
    • Qutub Minar - A 73-meter tall minaret, another UNESCO site.
    • Humayun's Tomb - A grand Mughal-era tomb that inspired the Taj Mahal's design.
    • Lotus Temple - A Bahai House of Worship known for its flower-like architecture.

  4. Udaipur, Rajasthan (The City of Lakes)


    • City Palace - A grand palace complex overlooking Lake Pichola.
    • Lake Pichola - A scenic artificial lake with boat rides and island palaces.
    • Jag Mandir - A palace built on an island in Lake Pichola.

  5. Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh


    • Ganges Ghats - Riverside steps used for rituals, bathing, and the famous Ganga Aarti ceremony.
    • Kashi Vishwanath Temple - One of the most sacred Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva.
    • Sarnath - Nearby site where Buddha gave his first sermon.

  6. Goa

    • Baga & Calangute Beaches - Popular beaches known for nightlife and water sports.
    • Basilica of Bom Jesus - A UNESCO-listed Portuguese-era church in Old Goa.
    • Fort Aguada - A 17th-century Portuguese fort with a lighthouse.

  7. Kochi, Kerala

    • Fort Kochi - Historic area with Chinese fishing nets and colonial architecture.
    • Alleppey Backwaters - Nearby houseboat cruises through palm-lined canals (day trip from Kochi).
    • Mattancherry Palace - A Portuguese-built palace with Kerala murals.

  8. Amritsar, Punjab

    • Golden Temple - The holiest Sikh shrine, famous for its gold-plated architecture and community kitchen.
    • Wagah Border - Site of the daily India-Pakistan border closing ceremony.
    • Jallianwala Bagh - A historic memorial garden.

  9. Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

    • Laxman Jhula & Ram Jhula - Iconic suspension bridges over the Ganges.
    • Triveni Ghat - A popular site for evening Ganga Aarti.
    • River Rafting - Rapids on the Ganges make this a top adventure sport destination.

  10. Leh, Ladakh

    • Pangong Lake - A high-altitude lake famous for its changing colors.
    • Leh Palace - A former royal palace overlooking the town.
    • Nubra Valley - Known for sand dunes and double-humped camels.

  11. Darjeeling, West Bengal

    • Tiger Hill - Famous sunrise viewpoint overlooking Kangchenjunga.
    • Darjeeling Himalayan Railway - A UNESCO World Heritage "Toy Train."
    • Tea Gardens - World-renowned Darjeeling tea plantations.

  12. Hampi, Karnataka

    • Virupaksha Temple - An active temple within the ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire.
    • Vittala Temple - Known for its iconic stone chariot.
    • Hampi Bazaar - Ancient marketplace ruins amid boulder landscapes.

  13. Port Blair, Andaman & Nicobar Islands

    • Radhanagar Beach - Consistently ranked among Asia's best beaches (Havelock Island).
    • Cellular Jail - A historic colonial-era prison, now a national memorial.
    • Scuba Diving & Snorkeling - Popular around Havelock and Neil Islands.

  14. Hyderabad, Telangana

    • Charminar - An iconic 16th-century mosque and monument with four grand arches.
    • Golconda Fort - A massive fort famous for its acoustics and diamond mining history.
    • Ramoji Film City - One of the world's largest film studio complexes, also a tourist attraction.
    • Hussain Sagar Lake - A large heart-shaped lake with a giant Buddha statue at its center.

  15. Chennai, Tamil Nadu

    • Marina Beach - One of the longest urban beaches in the world.
    • Kapaleeshwarar Temple - A vibrant Dravidian-style temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.
    • Fort St. George - The first English fortress in India, now housing a museum.
    • Mahabalipuram - Nearby UNESCO World Heritage Site known for ancient rock-cut temples and shore temples.

  16. Visakhapatnam (Vizag), Andhra Pradesh

    • Rushikonda Beach - A popular beach known for water sports.
    • Kailasagiri - A hilltop park offering panoramic views of the city and coastline.
    • Araku Valley - A scenic hill station nearby known for coffee plantations and tribal culture.
    • Submarine Museum (INS Kurusura) - A decommissioned submarine turned museum on the beach.
